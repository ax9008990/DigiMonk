Rumtek Monastery

“Preserving Buddha’s teachings with compassion”

Upcoming Events

Lhabab Duchen

This festival is held in October or November and celebrates Buddha's descent from the heavens. The monastery is often decorated with lamps and holds special prayers.

Date: Oct-Nov

Description

Rumtek Monastery, also known as the Dharmachakra Centre, is one of the largest and most important monasteries in Sikkim. It serves as the seat of the 16th Karmapa, the head of the Karma Kagyu lineage of Tibetan Buddhism. The monastery is renowned for its stunning architecture, vibrant prayer halls, and colorful rituals.

History

Originally built in the 16th century and rebuilt in the 1960s after the Chinese invasion of Tibet, Rumtek Monastery became the main center for the Karma Kagyu lineage outside Tibet. It has been a hub for Buddhist teachings, cultural preservation, and international Buddhist visitors.

Key Highlights

  • Located near Gangtok, surrounded by serene hills.
  • Houses sacred relics, thangkas, and statues.
  • Vibrant festivals like the annual Kagyed Cham dance.
  • Offers meditation retreats and teachings to monks and visitors.
  • Opened to the public in 1966.

Visiting Information

Open daily for visitors. Photography may be restricted inside prayer halls. Ideal time to visit is between October and March for festivals and pleasant weather.
